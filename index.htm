Sr. Experience Designer · Homeaway

How might you and I design simple, productive micro-experiences for humans online so that they enjoy more quality time offline?

It's my mission to answer that question with you

A primary issue with addictive smartphones and social media is that they’re designed to maximize engagement. Widespread, heavy usage of these tools is a only recent experiment, starting with the iPhone’s initial release in 2007. Early indications show that human health is taking a significant hit in various forms. Typical users of these technologies are acquiring a new array of social, physical, emotional and cognitive health issues. We’re becoming increasingly insecure, dumber, fatter, and less able to focus and read.

The root source of engagement-driven design involves usage-driven business models, which is common when businesses rely on advertising and consumption. However, now is best time for startups to shift towards human experience-first models. This approach aligns with the philosophy that long-term gains and relationships are sustainable when customers’ full 24-hour contexts and well-being are prioritized.

Designers and entrepenuers have the opportunity to create products that help people engage with experiences that truly matter: our offline, quality time spent with friends, family and the environment. How can we design tools that reduce our time online, so we spend more time living?

If you'd like to answer that question with me, please reach out.
